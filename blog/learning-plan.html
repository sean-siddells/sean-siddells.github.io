Sprint 2 Core Blog: Learning Plan

My long term goal is to be financially stable and work in a career that is not overly-physically or mentally exhaustive. I want whatever line of work I am in to feel balanced with quality time outside of work and for the culture of that field to be conducive with good health. I would like to use the skills I gain during this course to pursue a career in programming and to see if it fits well with my values. I’d like o work with my friends who have also gone through the Dev Academy and work on some personal projects together. I don’t really have a destination in mind.

I’m perceptive and adaptable when it comes to direction and feedback. I feel like I have good initiative when it comes to learning. I think motivation and focus can sometimes be a struggle for me, so setting good working practices and sticking to them will be a good exercise throughout the duration of this course. I think another limitation I’ve noticed I have is that I tend to wait too long to ask for help. I’m hoping that through the increased contact time with fellow classmates and facilitators I can combat this.

I’d like to improve my ability to talk in larger group settings, and as I’m sure there will be a few group presentations there should be ample opportunity for me to develop this at Dev Academy. I think the few human skills I would like to improve whilst at Dev Academy are my self awareness, ability to reflect and also to be kinder to myself. I tend to judge myself quite harshly and be overly self-critical. I think there is always room for improvement when it comes to communication and I hope to become more confident working with others and in the way that I carry myself.

I will manage my time by regularly setting achievable workloads and breaking it in small chunks with regular breaks/intervals between. I will work towards open-mindedness and kindness towards myself and others, and not be afraid to ask for help. I will try to communicate openly and be available to talk to my cohort and the facilitators. When things get stressful I will first step away and give myself some space from the source of what is frustrating me. I will talk to the people around me and also reach out to the cohort if this persists past a point where I feel as though I can handle it by myself.

I will use the slack channels provided to see if others can help me with whatever roadblocks I come across and try to do so before I’ve spent way too much time on said problem. I expect the facilitation team to be helpful in a non-judgemental way. I expect them to be understanding and respectful when people are being vulnerable.

I will possibly have a few hours of admin work to do per week which might have to be shifted into the evenings or split between several days once I start bootcamp.
