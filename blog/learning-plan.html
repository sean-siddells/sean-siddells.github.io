Sprint 2 Core Blog: Learning Plan

My long term goal is to be financially stable and work in a career that is not overly-physically or mentally exhaustive. I want whatever line of work I am in to feel balanced with quality time outside of work and for the culture of that field to be conducive with good health.

I’m perceptive and adaptable when it comes to direction and feedback. I feel like I have good initiative when it comes to learning. I think motivation and focus can sometimes be a struggle for me, so setting good working practices and sticking to them will be a good exercise throughout the duration of this course.

I’d like to improve my ability to talk in larger group settings, and as I’m sure there will be a few group presentations there should be ample opportunity for me to develop this at Dev Academy.

I will manage my time by regularly setting achievable workloads and breaking it in small chunks with regular breaks/intervals between. I will work towards open-mindedness and kindness towards myself and others, and not be afraid to ask for help. I will try to communicate openly and be available to talk to my cohort and the facilitators. When things get stressful I will first step away and give myself some space from the source of what is frustrating me. I will talk to the people around me and also reach out to the cohort if this persists past a point where I feel as though I can handle it by myself.

I will use the slack channels provided to see if others can help me with whatever roadblocks I come across and try to do so before I’ve spent way too much time on said problem.

I expect the facilitation team to be helpful in a non-judgemental way. I expect them to be understanding and respectful when people are being vulnerable.

I will possibly have a few hours of admin work to do per week which might have to be shifted into the evenings or split between several days once I start bootcamp.
