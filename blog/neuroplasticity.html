Neuroplasticity

Learning and the Brain

Neuroplasticity is the term used to describe the brain’s ability to constantly form new neural pathways and strengthen them over time. This means that learning is actually dependent on one’s willingness to practice and, as a result, develop these strong neural connections. Understanding neuroplasticity allows one to feel a greater sense of agency when it comes to changing their own behaviour and taking in new information. It challenges the belief of there being “natural” talent or there being a predisposition that means a person is better at something than another. The brain and therefore intelligence itself, is malleable.

I hope to be more persistent in my practice of things that I want to learn, and carry this concept of neuroplasticity as a motivator. Practicing the instruments that I want to get better at, learning new skills and perhaps being more patient with myself - as the new connections formed and neural pathways can only get stronger, right?

The Growth Mindset

The growth mindset is the belief that your ability can be improved. It is acknowledging that you may not be very competent at a certain task and understanding that it doesn’t reflect negatively on you, that you can overcome it. In Carol Dweck’s TED talk (linked below), she mentioned that the way we’ve raised children has created a drive for validation, as we tend to praise their results/grades. The idea that you should praise the process and not so much the grade/metric for intelligence was a really salient point. Encouraging persistence and curiosity as traits for children to carry into adulthood was something that made me think about my nephew and how his childhood will be.

Carol Dweck's Ted Talk

I will use this idea of the growth mindset especially when I feel out of my depth and remind myself that I can improve my ability instead of judging myself harshly. This combined with what I've learnt about neuroplasticity have highlighted the importance of taking good care of my physical health and being kind to myself when I feel like I'm stuck or struggling.
